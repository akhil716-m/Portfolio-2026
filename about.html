01 How it started

It started with curiosity, not a plan.

Growing up, technology was always around me — naturally, Computer Science felt like the next step.

But somewhere along the way, I realised I was far more interested in understanding people and solving problems than writing code.

During the COVID years, I started exploring. That's when I stumbled onto design — a field that blended creativity, technology, and human understanding in a way nothing else had.

What started as curiosity slowly became a passion, and eventually, a career.

02 How I think

I take things apart before I put them back together.

I've always enjoyed making sense of things.

Whether it's a product, a workflow, or a messy problem, my instinct is to break it down, question assumptions, and understand what's really happening beneath the surface.

Over time, this became the foundation of how I design.

The best solutions rarely come from adding more. They come from understanding more.

03 What I'm building towards

Looking for clarity, in increasingly complex places.

Today, I design products that balance simplicity, empathy, and thoughtful decision-making.

I'm particularly drawn to systems, AI, and complex problem spaces — the kind where design can pull clarity out of chaos.

As technology gets more powerful, I keep coming back to one question — how do we make it feel more human?
