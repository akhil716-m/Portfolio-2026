/03 How I Work

A working method, in five movements.

no double diamonds. just listening, sketching, shipping.
/01 — Listen

Find the real problem.

The brief is usually the symptom. The truth is buried in support tickets and a Slack thread from Q3.

/02 — Frame

Write before drawing.

If I can't explain it in two sentences, I haven't understood it. The pen comes after the paragraph.

/03 — Sketch

Make a lot. Keep little.

I'd rather throw away thirty versions than ship the first one that's "fine." Most of design is editing.

/04 — Build

Refine like it's a song.

Spacing, copy, motion, type. Each pass tightens the composition. The fourth one is usually the last.

A small belief · /04
Most fintech UI is afraid of having a personality. It hides behind grids and grey rectangles, as if seriousness and soul can't share a screen.

Money is emotional. Bills are emotional. Refunds are extremely emotional. So I design with that in mind — the form should feel as considered as the function.
A.M., somewhere between two coffees

Open to thoughtful conversations. I lean toward senior IC or design lead roles in fintech, SaaS, or health-tech — teams that ship steadily and iterate. Bengaluru-based, hybrid friendly.
/02
Do you take on freelance?
+
Selectively. I work best on focused projects — a payment flow, a system overhaul, a portfolio audit — over open-ended retainers.
/03
Why so much serif?
+
Most product sites look the same — same sans, same blue, same hero. Serifs feel like writing, not templating. They give the work a voice. That matters.
